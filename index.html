Understanding the Data

A Bitcoin Premium occurs when the local price of Bitcoin is higher than the global market price. This often happens in countries with currency instability because:

  • Capital Controls: Restrictions on moving money out of the country create a "desperation demand" for Bitcoin as an escape hatch.
  • Arbitrage Friction: It is difficult for traders to bring "new" Bitcoin into the country to lower the price, keeping premiums high.
  • Shadow Rates: Bitcoin prices often reflect the "true" street value of a currency, rather than the official government exchange rate.

Note: A higher premium doesn't always mean more people are using Bitcoin, but it often indicates how difficult it is for citizens to access stable value.
